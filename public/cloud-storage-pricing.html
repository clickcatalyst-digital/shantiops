Three real options — not just one

Three of the five providers each win on something different: the lowest bill, never being charged for downloads, or fitting neatly next to software you may already run elsewhere. Here's the honest case for each.

B2

Backblaze B2 — the cheapest, month after month

At your size, B2 costs the least of the five — a little less than Cloudflare, and much less than Amazon, Microsoft, or Google. It also won't charge you extra for downloading files, as long as what you download each month stays under three times what you have stored — which your numbers comfortably do. The catch: B2 only sells storage. It doesn't run the software behind your CRM or offer the extra cloud services the big three do, so it works best as a standalone filing cabinet, not as part of a bigger cloud setup.

R2

Cloudflare R2 — you'll never be charged to download a file

R2 costs a bit more than B2 to store your files each month, but downloading them is always free — no ratio to track, no fine print. If your team's downloads ever grow faster than planned, or you'd simply rather not think about it, this removes that worry for good. It's run by Cloudflare, a large and widely-known company, which may feel more familiar to a review committee than Backblaze. The catch: at your current numbers, you'd likely pay somewhat more overall for a guarantee your usage may not end up needing.

S3

Amazon S3 — the safe choice if you're already inside a big cloud

S3 costs more than both B2 and R2 at your size, but it's the natural pick if the software running your CRM or ERP already lives on Amazon's, Microsoft's, or Google's cloud. Keeping files on the same cloud as the software that uses them avoids extra delay and avoids paying two companies to move data between them. It also carries the deepest paperwork and certifications for handling sensitive data, which matters more as a business grows. The catch: for a small team storing mostly documents and backups, you'd be paying big-cloud prices for a benefit — same-cloud convenience — that only shows up once your software actually lives there.

There's no wrong choice among these three — it comes down to what your team already runs, and how much you'd rather never think about download costs again. The choice is yours; the next section lays out the honest downside of each so you're deciding with full information.

The honest downside of each option

Every option here trades something away. None of these are dealbreakers for a small team storing mostly documents and backups — but you should pick with your eyes open.

Backblaze B2

Storage only — nothing elseCheck first

B2 doesn't run software or offer extra cloud services alongside storage. If your CRM or ERP ever needs to run inside the same cloud as its files, B2 can't host that side of it — it only ever works as a standalone filing cabinet.

No India-based data centerCheck first

Amazon, Microsoft, and Google all have data centers in India. Backblaze doesn't. If it ever becomes a requirement to legally keep your files inside India — a government contract, certain regulated data — that's a full switch, not a setting.

Cloudflare R2

You pay more to insure against a risk you may not haveWatch this

R2's storage costs roughly double B2's, in exchange for a guarantee — free downloads, always — that your current usage doesn't actually need. B2's free-download allowance already comfortably covers your numbers, so today that guarantee isn't buying you much.

A newer service, with a few short hiccupsWorth knowing

Cloudflare's storage product has had a handful of brief outages over the past year — none that lost data, but worth knowing before treating any provider as bulletproof. Backblaze and the big three each have a longer track record specifically for storage.

Amazon S3

The most expensive of the three, for your numbersWatch this

At 1 TB, S3 costs roughly 15 times what B2 does and about 7 times what R2 does. That premium buys same-cloud convenience and deeper compliance paperwork — value you'll only see if your software actually runs on Amazon's cloud.

Downloading files still isn't freeLow impact

Unlike R2's always-free downloads, S3 charges once you're past the first 100 GB you download each month. At your volumes that adds up a little, though it's modest next to what you'd already be paying for storage there.

The quick way to decide

  • Your CRM/ERP software already runs on Amazon, Microsoft, or Google's cloud → use that same company's storage (S3, Azure, or GCS). Splitting storage and software across two different clouds costs more in delay and complexity than it saves in dollars.
  • Keeping files inside India could ever become a requirement → Amazon, Microsoft, or Google. Backblaze and Cloudflare can't offer this today.
  • You'd rather never think about download costs again → Cloudflare R2. Costs a little more, removes the question permanently.
  • None of the above apply, and you just want the lowest bill → Backblaze B2 — and put what you save toward a proper backup and version-history layer, which you'll want regardless of which provider you choose.

What this estimate does — and doesn't — cover

  • These are estimates, not quotes. All five providers price by region, redundancy option, and sometimes negotiated/committed-use discounts — actual invoices can run higher or lower than shown here. Use the linked calculators for a binding number.
  • Recovery-within-a-day (what you selected) needs versioning or a backup layer on top of raw storage — that's a separate line item depending on the backup tool you use, not a per-GB storage rate.
  • Compliance: you flagged this as private/internal data only — all five providers scramble your files so they're unreadable to anyone but your account, both while stored and while being transferred, which is sufficient for that classification.
  • Rates shown are US-region list pricing. If you host in an India region, Amazon/Microsoft/Google's rates run somewhat higher than the figures above; B2 and R2 price the same worldwide, so their numbers hold. The linked calculators will settle it for your chosen region.
  • Figures exclude taxes and support-plan fees. The small per-action fees some providers charge for uploading, downloading, or listing files are now $0 on B2's standard usage (changed May 2026), and fall inside R2's free monthly allowance at your volumes — so for the two cheapest options, this genuinely isn't a hidden cost.
